IMPLEMENTATION OF SALESFORCE:
SELECTION OF CRM SYSTEM

(part 2 of 4)
• Date: December 2020 • Estimated reading time: 7 minutes

Dmitri Leichik

CEO, medstifter af Twistellar

Twistellar would like to start a series of articles dedicated to implementation of Salesforce. This is the 2nd part of 4. Follow us on LinkedIn or Facebook so you won't miss a new article. Read the 1st part here.

In the first part we were talking about selection of CRM and what the criteria for choosing one are. We went deep into the topic of Flexibility and how it affects the work flow of the company. In this part we are going to discuss the importance of Scalability and what are the initial costs of licenses and implementation.

Scalability

Scalability is the attribute of a platform that describes its expansion potential.

Numerous of "IF" you need to make sure that your CRM will handle it without performance degradation:

  • If you're going to grow your business, when more people start working with the system
  • if you're going to automate additional functions in future
  • if the number of operations will grow

If not - you'll have to either refuse further digitalization, or will have to think about switching the platform (which is usually much more expensive than implementing a proper one from scratch). But - that's only IF you're going to need that.

Platform Expansion Example

What does scalability look like from Salesforce perspective?

We've seen marvelous examples. Among our projects there are cases when 10 000 licenses are used in one Salesforce instance by just one company (yes, we work with the world's biggest brands, Fortune 500 members).

In another project we managed to achieve the performance of 700 transactions with heavy interconnected backend calculations for a number of related objects for each of them(e-commerce commodity trade operations) in one second on force.com platform. That's over 15 billion operations a year! And Salesforce works in such a way that in order to get access and start using all this power - you need just one enterprise license, which is huge!

With Salesforce you can be sure that you can start with just basic licenses and basic functionality - and you'll never have a reason to switch to another system.

With Salesforce you can be sure that you can start with just basic licenses and basic functionality - and you'll never have a reason to switch to another system. Salesforce will be able to grow with your company, and you'll never have to take care of any IT infrastructure yourself. Of course, this argument is meaningful if you're going to scale your company. So Salesforce is also a great selection for startups.

What are the main challenges of switching your CRM to Salesforce in future?

Usually the biggest challenges lie around data migration. We've seen many cases when the matter of proper data cleaning and preparing to be loaded in the updated data model takes the amount of efforts comparable with the configuration of the whole system itself. Effectively, that means that instead of investing in the configuration of the scalable system initially, you pay twice in future.

However, of course, for many companies the flexibility and performance of smaller systems is completely enough for their goals, so Salesforce is not the only choice out there. You just need to know and consider the decision making factors.

And what's the main consideration of NOT selecting Salesforce as your CRM platform? Pricing, of course, so read further.

Enterprise-grade Salesforce Landscape

Licenses and Implementation Costs

Salesforce is considered to be one of the most expensive systems on the market. True? Yes and no. If you directly compare the cost of Sales Cloud Enterprise Edition licenses of Salesforce and, let's say, something like Pipedrive or SugarCRM, then definitely Salesforce will be more expensive. But if you're really interested in getting the maximum out of Salesforce for the optimal budget, you should remember of Community Licenses.

Also make sure that your implementation partner will help you negotiate with Salesforce to get the best possible personal offer and special pricing for licenses, if applicable.

We've already shared an article on community licenses - so please take a look. Overall, there're ways of saving costs on licenses in Salesforce, that should be considered. However, as mentioned before, most probably with the direct comparison of your budget for Salesforce implementation and some other system, Salesforce will still be more expensive.

But let's rethink what that means from a business perspective. We always recommend our customers not to compare the absolute cost of a CRM platform. Let's better think of the impact of a CRM project on your business, as any investment should end up in sufficient ROI.

Let's think of a business that gets an average 10K revenue on one employee (not a big amount really, probably typical for service-type companies, with relatively high margins). What would a CEO expect from CRM implementation? Of course - productivity increase. Let's say that we're aiming at 20% increase (Salesforce claims that you can achieve much more). If we're successful with our project, we should expect 20% growth of sales, which would make 12K revenue on one employee. I think it's obvious that those additional 2K monthly will definitely cover the cost of a Salesofrce license, and the implementation cost, distributed over even 1 year. From that perspective it's not THAT important, whether a CRM license will cost 100 or 50, is it? Much more important would be to focus on the success of the project overall, and mitigation of risks that could lead it to failure.

Also an important consideration is to make sure that your implementation partner will help you negotiate with Salesforce to get the best possible personal offer and special pricing for licenses, if applicable.

Project cost depending on licenses combination

As for implementation costs, it's a more complicated matter

On the Internet you could easily find package prices for implementation of Sales Cloud or Service Cloud or Marketing Cloud. It's quite easy to provide "average" figures. However, the actual spread of the project costs varies greatly depending on the company.

We had projects dedicated to implementation of a basic CRM functionality (sales pipeline), that cost hundreds of thousands. And on the contrary, for a smaller company we could suggest configuring the sales pipeline at just 5K. That's just because people use the same words to call completely different projects and scope.

The most typical components of CRM implementation that impact the implementation cost

Data model

To represent all the entities involved in your business processes may require expanding standard objects with all the necessary attributes, validations, relationships and automations not available out of the box even in Salesforce. To customize a typical object properly can take 4-12 hours easily.

Permissions and screen layouts

If you need to present information in a different way for different people, the definition of all the rules and the looks of all the screens may take time. You can easily spend 80-100 hours on permissions and layouts only, in the most complicated cases.

UI (Front-end) customization

You may decide that the standard Salesforce interface, despite all its flexibility and configuration capabilities, doesn't suit. And indeed, we can build any interface you want, but that's quite a costly development. One custom screen could take from 24 to 100 hours depending on the functionality.

Backend automations and integrations

That's usually quite a laborious part. To use Salesforce and not use its integration capabilities is not a very wise choice - especially if your company has a lot of legacy systems and requires unification of data and generic management and reporting capabilities. Salesforce can act as a management system to drive all other components of your IT infrastructure. A typical integration may take 60-100 hours, with more challenging integrations requiring hundreds of hours.

Of course, there're a lot of apps on AppExchange that can help to avoid investments in custom development. And professional implementation partners happily recommend the ways to save costs with that.

The best way to approach a project (and save costs) is to develop the project in stages, and first implement and adopt something viable, collect feedback from users, and then build up on that.

So, what are the most important decision making factors for selecting your CRM system:

  • direct comparison of the absolute cost of implementing Salesforce and other systems will most probably not be in the favor of Salesforce, however, as indicated above - it's not that important. And though you can probably achieve what you need with a more simple system, selecting Salesforce greatly decreases the risks related to the technological factors
  • what is more important, is to ensure that the implementation of a CRM system is planned as an iterative process with stages - that will decrease the overhead of configuring unneeded functionality and improve adoption. However, it's also crucial that the overall vision of the project and its goals are defined initially, as otherwise it will be easy to lose the track of required automations and fail the project
